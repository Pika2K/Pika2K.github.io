Hong Kong Heritage List #1: The Murray House

The Murray House is a prominent landmark in Hong Kong that was originally built in 1844 as officers' quarters for the British army.
The building was originally located in the Central district but was dismantled in 1982 to make way for a new Bank of China tower and moved to its current location in Stanley in 1990.
During the Second World War, the Murray House served as a military hospital. After the war, it was converted into government offices and later into a luxury hotel.
The building has since been refurbished and now houses restaurants and shops. Its historical significance and unique architecture make it a popular tourist attraction in Hong Kong.
However,its original classification as a Grade 1 building has not been transferred to the rebuilt building as the current Murray House is considered a relocation project that does not meet the international standard for heritage preservation. For reference, the guidelines and criteria conditions for the evaluation of historical assets are as below:
- Historic and aesthetic significance
- Scientific or research significance
- Social and spiritual significance
